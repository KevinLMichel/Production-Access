Laughter is medicine for the heart.<br />
Do not be solemn beyond measure – the gods themselves enjoy a jest.<br />
A moment of honest mirth can dispel a mountain of sorrow.<br />
Learn to laugh at yourself kindly; it is a sign of wisdom.<br />
A cheerful heart draws people as a fire draws the cold.<br />
So long as your laughter harms no one and lightens the spirit, it is blessed.<br />
In laughter we find our common humanity.<br />
Even in hard times, a bit of humor is a lamp in the dark.<br />
Ma’at smiles upon those who balance earnestness with joy.
