Do not poke your nose into every quarrel or secret.<br />
One who grabs a stray dog by the ears invites a bite; so does one who leaps into others’ disputes invite trouble.<br />
Offer advice when asked, not before.<br />
Keep confidence with what is told in trust – gossip is a knife that cuts both speaker and subject.<br />
Mind your own tasks and let others manage theirs unless great harm would follow your silence.<br />
By avoiding meddling, you keep your peace and earn trust.<br />
The quiet bystander often sees more clearly than the noisy interferer.
