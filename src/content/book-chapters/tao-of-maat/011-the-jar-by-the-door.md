At the door of a humble house sits a jar of cool water.<br />
Each morning the householder fills it, though no traveler may come.<br />
He asks nothing in return; his heart is light knowing a wanderer’s thirst might be eased.<br />
Charity is a quiet ripple in the pond of eternity.<br />
The giver, the receiver, and the water itself are blessed in the act.<br />
In giving, he serves Ma’at; in caring, he becomes part of the divine balance.
