The sage of Ma’at lives quietly and without strife.<br />
They nurture justice in their heart and need no boast on their lips.<br />
They step aside from credit, letting good deeds be their own reward.<br />
Untroubled by anger and untouched by greed, the sage moves through life like water: gentle and unstoppable.<br />
They give without keeping account, lead without dominating, teach without condescension.

When success comes, they smile and move on; when failure comes, they learn and move on.<br />
In company, they are gracious; in solitude, they are content.<br />
The sage carries Ma’at in their breast as a living flame.<br />
By their presence, the world is made lighter, yet they claim no glory.<br />
They are like a tree giving shade – all benefit, and the tree does not proclaim its virtue.<br />
Empty of ego and full of compassion, the sage lives in harmony with all.<br />
In them, the Way of Ma’at finds its purest expression.
