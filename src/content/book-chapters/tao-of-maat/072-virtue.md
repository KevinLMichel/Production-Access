Do good for its own sake, not for praise or gain.<br />
A righteous act loses grace if done for show.<br />
The hidden good you do, the kindness no one sees – these are the purest offerings to Ma’at.<br />
The wind does not applaud the tree for giving shade, yet the traveler is still comforted.<br />
Do right even when no reward is in sight; that is when virtue truly lives.<br />
Fame is fickle and gold loses its luster, but quiet goodness endures in the heart.<br />
The soul nourished by virtue needs no audience.<br />
Be your own witness to your integrity, and let that be enough.
