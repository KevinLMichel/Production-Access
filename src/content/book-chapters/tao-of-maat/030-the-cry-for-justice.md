In a hall of granite and gold, a peasant stood trembling yet unyielding.<br />
He had been robbed by a wicked hand, and no one would hear him.<br />
Day after day, he returned to the gates of the great house; his voice grew hoarse, yet stronger with truth.<br />
The eloquence of the wronged pierced the steward’s heart, and at last the scales were set right.<br />
Do not underestimate the power of a single honest voice.<br />
Ma’at walks with the one who stands up for the right, even when he stands alone.
