Through night he trudged until first blush did bloom,<br />
With dawn’s faint rose caressing eastern sky.<br />
The desert’s edge gave way as sand made room<br />
For firmer earth where life could multiply.<br />
Scrub brush and acacia in clusters spread,<br />
A hint that verdant lands at last lie nigh.<br />
The pilgrim’s weary feet now lighter tread,<br />
Buoyed by hope of water and reprieve,<br />
And inner thirst to see what lay ahead.<br />
Soon purpling hills did dawn’s hues receive,<br />
And beyond them, hints of spires glinting bright—<br />
A city ancient, he could scarce believe.

He crested one last dune in golden light,<br />
And lo, before him stretched a valley green,<br />
Cradling a river shimmering in might.<br />
Beside its banks, a marble city seen<br />
Whose walls and towers reached for azure dome,<br />
Crowned by a massive temple pure and sheen.<br />
The pilgrim’s heart quickened at that sight home;<br />
For in the grandeur of that mystic place,<br />
He sensed he neared the source for which he’d roam.<br />
As he descended, sun’s warmth did embrace<br />
His aching limbs and heart’s uncertain beat,<br />
Filling him with new vigor, joy, and grace.

A winding path led down to where the sweet<br />
Waters of the broad river gently flowed.<br />
A stone bridge arched invitingly to meet<br />
The city gates where giant statues showed:<br />
Carved cherubim and beasts of mythic lore,<br />
Wardens of secrets behind walls bestowed.<br />
But by the river’s bank he paused before<br />
Crossing, drawn by laughter and singing clear,<br />
Carried by morning’s breeze from further shore.<br />
Turning aside, he sought the sound so dear,<br />
And soon came upon children at their play,<br />
Dancing in shallows where the water’s near.

They splashed and giggled in the new-born day,<br />
Chasing reeds fashioned as makeshift ships,<br />
Their faces bright as dawn in merry fray.<br />
Their garb was humble linen, girded hips,<br />
And skin of varying hues as earth can show—<br />
Some bronzed by sun, some fair as lunar eclipse.<br />
One boy caught sight of the pilgrim and so<br />
Stood still, causing the others pause to gaze.<br />
The wanderer smiled and bent his head low.<br />
“Good morrow, children. What tune do you raise<br />
That gladdens the heart of a traveler lone?<br />
Whence comes your joy in these early sun rays?”

A girl stepped forth with a flower crown sewn<br />
Of river lilies in her tangled hair,<br />
She offered one to him in gentle tone.<br />
“Mister, we greet you! We have no despair<br />
In our fair city, which we call The Beloved—<br />
All who enter find hope and solace there.<br />
We sing songs that our elders long have loved,<br />
Of heroes, of angels, of saints and kings,<br />
But most of all, of Love that all behoved.”<br />
Another child piped up, excitement brings:<br />
“Yes! You must come. Today is a feast day!<br />
In the great Temple the high choir sings.”

The pilgrim felt a tug as if to say<br />
This chance meeting was more than happenstance.<br />
He graciously took the lily bouquet,<br />
Thanking each child with a kind, warm glance.<br />
Then taking leave, he crossed the arcing bridge,<br />
Towards city gates in reverent advance.<br />
Massive doors stood open; above, on ridge<br />
Of the arch, engraved words in script antique:<br />
“Welcome ye thirsty, hereto knowledge’s ridge.”<br />
Guards were none but stone statues mystique,<br />
Their visages calm with wisdom and peace,<br />
No swords, for none were needed to keep pique.

He stepped inside; marvels did not cease.<br />
Wide boulevards with colonnades aligned,<br />
Lining streets in harmony as if fleece<br />
Of marble softened outlines well-designed.<br />
Gardens spilled fragrance into open squares<br />
Where fountains leaped in arcs silver-refined.<br />
Folk ambled gently, no rushing affairs;<br />
Their faces content, eyes bright with purpose clear,<br />
As if each soul a hidden radiance bears.<br />
Men, women, and children moved without fear;<br />
Some in lively chat, others deep in thought,<br />
All neighbors in goodness sincere.

At the city’s heart, dominating wrought,<br />
A temple soared of alabaster white,<br />
With golden dome with morning’s fire caught.<br />
Courtyards wide encircled it, trees bedight<br />
With blossoms of myriad hue and shape,<br />
Perfuming breeze with sacred delight.<br />
Our pilgrim realized as jaw agape<br />
That his steps had led him to a domain<br />
Seemingly earthly, yet as vision drape<br />
It felt heaven-kissed, free of sorrow’s stain.<br />
He wondered if in truth it was utopia,<br />
Or a test disguised in pleasure’s fane.

Seeking guidance and to quell myopia,<br />
He approached an elder under an oak,<br />
A man reading scrolls of wide cyclopaedia.<br />
“Sir,” he ventured in gentle, cautious spoke,<br />
“Pray tell me of this city wondrous fair;<br />
I am a stranger, guided by some stroke<br />
Of providence, drawn by children to care<br />
That this day is sacred—some feast or rite.<br />
What ethos rules this place beyond compare?”<br />
The old man lifted eyes with friendly light,<br />
“Welcome, friend. You’ve arrived on hallowed day:<br />
We celebrate The Festival of Sight.

Our city, Beloved, by name we say,<br />
Holds dear all seekers who yearn for the True;<br />
We honor all paths that lead through dismay<br />
Into hopeful virtue and knowledge too.<br />
Our founders were mystics, sages, and seers<br />
From lands far-flung, each a different hue<br />
Of faith and wisdom. They shed mutual tears<br />
Over humanity’s endless discord,<br />
And dreamed a city where love endures.<br />
Together they labored, one mind, accord,<br />
To build this haven for hearts and minds;<br />
The Temple you see is their great reward.

In that Temple we keep the flame that binds,<br />
Kindled from altars of East and of West,<br />
A fire eternal where truth one finds.<br />
Today, as dawn gilds our city’s crest,<br />
We gather, all citizens hand in hand,<br />
To seek inner sight and to greet each guest.<br />
You come fortuitous, as if by plan,<br />
For all are welcome at the Feast of Sight;<br />
Join us and see what our rites command.”<br />
The pilgrim listened, heart warmed by insight,<br />
For this city seemed embodiment plain<br />
Of what he glimpsed in last moment’s midnight.

He thanked the elder, then strolled to the fane,<br />
Temple steps of marble smooth and pristine,<br />
Mingling now with a crowd in joyful train.<br />
People greeted him kindly, faces serene,<br />
As though a long-lost friend had come back home;<br />
He felt acceptance like a gentle rain.<br />
Ascending stairs with many a smooth dome<br />
He passed through columns in open foyer,<br />
Into a hall wide as starry sky’s roam.<br />
High above soared a dome that would inspire<br />
The greatest cathedrals to humility:<br />
At its center an oculus of fire.

Sunlight poured through that eye, nobility<br />
Cascading golden beams across the space,<br />
Illuminating murals of nobility:<br />
Scenes of compassion, courage and grace,<br />
Drawn from scriptures and epics of each way—<br />
Krishna and Arjuna in war’s embrace,<br />
Abraham and Isaac, moment they pray,<br />
Buddha under Bodhi tree radiant,<br />
Christ on the Cross forgiving in decay,<br />
Rumi with reed pen swirling aspirant,<br />
Lao Tzu wandering with water’s ease,<br />
Joan in flames unyielding and defiant.

Every pillar told stories such as these,<br />
In art entwining world’s faith and lore,<br />
No one triumphant over others to seize<br />
Pride of place—all were honored at core.<br />
The pilgrim beheld these wonders in awe,<br />
Feeling each story a truth to explore,<br />
And in each figure’s eyes a love he saw,<br />
One source behind their myriad eyes aflame:<br />
As if one Beloved did through all draw.<br />
Then the assembly gathered, and words came,<br />
A hush as robed choirs took their rightful place,<br />
Preparing the hymn for festival’s aim.

A hush of expectancy filled the space;<br />
The pilgrim slid into a pew of oak,<br />
Beside a family with kindness on face.<br />
Then a single bell in the silence broke—<br />
Not loud, but with resonance pure and deep,<br />
Like heart’s own note in solitude awoke.<br />
Three times it tolled as souls willing to weep<br />
At truth’s beauty felt tears rise unbidden;<br />
With each toll, silence seemed deeper to seep.<br />
Then, out of hush, a soprano, hidden,<br />
Rose in chant, in Latin or tongue unknown,<br />
A plaintive call as if to Eden.

As her voice soared, more voices were grown<br />
Each after each in different dialect,<br />
One in Sanskrit, one Arabic intone,<br />
Greek, Hebrew, Chinese—each did interject<br />
Their praise of the Highest in native tongue,<br />
Yet strangely harmonized as if perfect.<br />
A melody thus deftly strung<br />
Enraptured all, including pilgrim’s heart;<br />
He felt as if heaven’s gate had unclung.<br />
The hymn built in power, each voice a part<br />
Of an ever-rising wave of the soul,<br />
Crashing in unison at chorus’s start.

Then all tongues together, without control,<br />
Sang one phrase clear that all could understand—<br />
“Open our eyes to the Beloved Whole!”<br />
The dome above seemed to shake as if fanned<br />
By wings of seraphs echoing that prayer;<br />
Light from the oculus grew and did expand.<br />
The pilgrim felt love beyond all compare<br />
Pour from that sound and light into his core,<br />
Washing away every lingering care.<br />
He closed his eyes, tears flowing more and more,<br />
And in his mind’s eye saw a vision bright:<br />
A figure, faceless, opening a door.

The figure beckoned him in shining white,<br />
And beyond the door lay a boundless field<br />
Where golden lilies swayed in heavenly light.<br />
He stepped through in spirit, senses unsealed,<br />
And walked amidst that endless living gold,<br />
Feeling all secrets of existence yield.<br />
In each lily, a story would unfold,<br />
Of a soul’s journey or an atom’s dance;<br />
In each petal, infinity he could behold.<br />
He heard silent music and watched entranced<br />
As the sky rippled color never seen,<br />
And beings moved beyond comprehension’s glance.

Then came a presence gentle and serene—<br />
The sense of an embrace without form,<br />
As though the field itself in love did lean.<br />
Words unspoken draped him cozy and warm:<br />
“You are beloved, child of dust and star,<br />
No longer roam in fear of worldly storm.<br />
Within you burns our flame, near or far;<br />
Your hunger is our signal guiding true,<br />
Through strife and night to where the good things are.<br />
Know that you seek us as we seek you,<br />
In hidden ways through conscience, through longing,<br />
Each moment a string in love’s element new.”

The pilgrim soul replied, heartbeats thronging,<br />
“Tell me, who are you?” though he felt he knew—<br />
The answer came like a bell song ringing:<br />
“I am the Beloved and the Seeker too,<br />
Alpha and Omega of each quest,<br />
The emptiness that yields fullness anew.<br />
I have been with you when you thought suppressed,<br />
In forest dark and desert of despair,<br />
I am your hunger and your needed rest.<br />
All forms of the Good, True, Beautiful fair<br />
Are facets of me calling from above,<br />
Yet also the void wherein these appear.”

At that, the pilgrim felt overwhelming love,<br />
A union as if every vein did pulse<br />
With life eternal, below and above.<br />
He wanted to linger in that impulse,<br />
Yet as the choir’s final chord did peal,<br />
The vision faded back to temple’s halls.<br />
He opened his eyes, needing time to feel<br />
The fullness of what had been shown within.<br />
Congregants sat in silence after zeal;<br />
Not a rustle disturbed peace genuine.<br />
At length the officiant, an elder wise,<br />
Gave gentle benediction to all kin:

“May the Beloved open all our eyes<br />
To see beyond forms to the heart of grace,<br />
And through emptiness let true sight arise.”<br />
The assembly stirred, joy on every face,<br />
And slowly filed to courtyards bright outside<br />
To break the fast with bread and fruit apace.<br />
The pilgrim moved as if mystified,<br />
In daze of bliss from vision’s high bequest,<br />
Unsure if he dreamed or crossed some divide.<br />
As he wandered out, hand on heart impressed,<br />
He nearly stumbled into one waiting:<br />
The Sufi woman statue brought to life, no less.

It was she—no longer stone, but breathing,<br />
The same veiled form he’d glimpsed in desert hall;<br />
Now flesh and blood, with kind eyes all-seeing.<br />
He caught his breath at the marvel and awe.<br />
She smiled beneath her veil and bowed her head.<br />
“I greet you again, seeker who saw<br />
Our counsel in the sand. You have been led<br />
Truly, as your vision in song revealed.<br />
Yet your journey goes on, not finished or dead.<br />
The hunger that stirred you, that truth unsealed,<br />
Must now be tempered into living flame—<br />
A fire of love with integrity sealed.

Not for your sake alone is wisdom’s name<br />
Engraved upon your heart by mystic light,<br />
But to serve others caught in fear or shame.<br />
Beloved City gave you a taste bright<br />
Of unity and peace which souls can reach;<br />
But beyond our gates roars the endless night<br />
Of world’s sorrow, conflict, folly, and breach.<br />
Many hunger still for what you have seen,<br />
Blind in the darkness with no guiding speech.<br />
To rest here in bliss, while outside they keen,<br />
Would squander grace into stagnation’s mire,<br />
For wisdom unused withers, dull of sheen.”

The pilgrim listened, a chord struck in lyre<br />
Of his conscience; he felt truth in her word.<br />
Part of him longed to rest, a selfish desire,<br />
To dwell forever where his heart was stirred<br />
By love and knowledge weaving sweet accord.<br />
But the image of others lost, unheard,<br />
Wandering as he had in darkness, roared<br />
Through memory—a call he could not blight.<br />
He remembered phantoms and forest cord,<br />
His own despair before he had the sight.<br />
How could he feast while others starve in gloom?<br />
To share this grace felt a sacred right.

She saw resolve like dawn on him illume,<br />
And gently placed a hand upon his arm.<br />
“North of here beyond the valley’s bloom,<br />
Past mountain crags where winds blow fierce alarm,<br />
Lie lands wracked by war, ignorance, and grief.<br />
We send envoys, healing hands to calm<br />
And guide those willing toward soul’s relief.<br />
Go, join their number, for your journey calls<br />
Not to an end in comfort’s motif<br />
But to service where divine duty falls.<br />
Before you depart, take this gift from me—”<br />
From her robe she withdrew two shawls.

One shawl was earth-brown, plain for all to see,<br />
The other sky-blue embroidered with gold.<br />
She offered the first: “Wear this humbly.<br />
In rough lands you’ll tread, blend as common mold,<br />
A simple monk or healer to their eyes.<br />
It’s through humility your strength takes hold.”<br />
The second shawl she held with care precise:<br />
“This one keep hidden till the moment true,<br />
It bears enchantments of the mystic wise.<br />
When despair encircles and confounds you,<br />
Unfurl it to remember the Highest,<br />
The Beloved’s light will then surround you.”

He accepted both with bowed head in trust,<br />
Grateful beyond words for her wise accord.<br />
“Thank you,” he breathed. “I will do as you must<br />
If it pleases the Beloved, my lord.”<br />
She chuckled softly, a melody bright,<br />
“Service pleases the One without reward.<br />
Go well, dear seeker, walk within the Light.”<br />
With that, she turned and merged into the crowd,<br />
A single dove took flight in sky’s delight.<br />
The pilgrim stood, heart steady now and vowed<br />
To carry forth the unity he’d learned<br />
Into the broken world beyond this cloud.

He made his way out gates where first he yearned,<br />
Crossing the bridge from paradise to pale<br />
Wild lands beyond, where cruelty burned.<br />
As he left, the children he’d met set sail<br />
On their reed boats waved from the river’s brim,<br />
And he smiled and waved back without fail.<br />
Thus from beloved city’s vision dim<br />
He journeyed onward, purpose newly cast:<br />
To quench thirst of others filled to the brim.<br />
This moment closes as he travels fast<br />
To distant valleys where suffering calls,<br />
With love aflame to heal wounds of the past.
