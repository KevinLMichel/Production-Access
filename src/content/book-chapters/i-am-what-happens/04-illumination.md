Climbing the heights where the air runs thin,<br />
Our pilgrim forged on through rocky ascent.<br />
The path was steep and narrow as a pin,<br />
Yet drawn as if by fate, he did not relent.<br />
Days of travel and nights under cold sky<br />
He pressed upward, by inner zeal content.<br />
As altitude soared, clouds drifting by,<br />
The world below a patchwork distant dream,<br />
He felt closer to truth he sensed nearby.<br />
At last one dawn’s light cast a golden beam<br />
On a plateau high above verdant earth,<br />
A summit plateau by heaven’s seam.

Arriving at midday, a curious mirth<br />
Seized him: the summit was not barren stone<br />
But a meadow with flowers bursting forth.<br />
Alpine blooms of every color known<br />
Danced in sunlight under crystal air,<br />
As if paradise on mount had been sown.<br />
At the far end, a cliff dropped off somewhere<br />
Beyond mortal sight—an abyss sublime.<br />
At center, an oak tree ancient and fair<br />
Spread its mighty branches, defying time.<br />
Its trunk gnarled and knotted with scars of age,<br />
Yet leaves fresh green as in youthful prime.

Under this tree, as if hermitage,<br />
Sat a solitary figure cross-legged.<br />
Robed in simple white garb of a sage,<br />
Back turned to pilgrim, wholly unalleged.<br />
Sensing presence, the figure spoke calm:<br />
“Welcome, traveler. Your path was rugged.<br />
Come, rest at the tree; find peace and balm.”<br />
The pilgrim obeyed, quietly drawing near,<br />
Feeling sweet solace as if wrapped in psalm.<br />
He sat by the stranger with love sincere,<br />
Noticing now an old man with shaved head,<br />
Beard white, eyes closed as if truth were clear.

Moments passed in silence, nothing said,<br />
But a profound communication flowed.<br />
The pilgrim felt as if mind had been read,<br />
That this sage without speaking all bestowed<br />
Comfort, acknowledgment, understanding.<br />
At length the old man’s eyes opened, glowed<br />
With bright hazel light, compassion blending.<br />
“You have come far, seeker of the true,<br />
Through forest dark, desert, city tending<br />
The suffering of others with heart anew.<br />
Tell me, what is it that you now desire,<br />
Here at world’s roof beneath heaven’s blue?”

The pilgrim thought deeply, as was required.<br />
What did he yet seek? He’d learned and done<br />
So much. Yet something deeper still aspired.<br />
“I have beheld unity, nearly won<br />
Peace within and without by grace and trial.<br />
I have served love under moon and sun.<br />
Yet still, O master, remains one dial<br />
I cannot yet read—the ultimate goal,<br />
To truly be that love without denial.<br />
My hunger for truth has carved out a soul,<br />
But I fear to carve God from my craving<br />
Is folly. How to fully be made whole?”

The sage smiled gentle at this engraving<br />
Of doubt laid bare by the pilgrim’s artless heart.<br />
“You fear your hunger’s idol might be braving<br />
A false god? That by your own hand and art<br />
You’ve only shaped illusion to worship,<br />
Not the reality behind the chart?”<br />
The pilgrim nodded, each word like a whip<br />
On self-honesty flayed raw but cleansing.<br />
“Yes, teacher. I’ve seen through lies and hardship,<br />
Yet how do I know if I am sensing<br />
The true Beloved, or just my mind’s shape?<br />
Yearning is deep, but truth needs discerning.”

The old man closed eyes briefly, then drape<br />
One hand on the ground, the other upturned,<br />
As if bridging earth and sky in gape.<br />
“Your journey’s lessons have by now affirmed<br />
That paradox and union mark the Way:<br />
In emptiness, form; through fire, life returned;<br />
In serving others, self finds light of day.<br />
Now comes final trial, subtle and profound:<br />
To lose even the hunger that led your stay.<br />
Not to kill it, but to let it drown<br />
In the ocean that is the Beloved’s grace—<br />
To merge the seeker and sought, self unbound.”

He rose smoothly to feet, in motion grace,<br />
And beckoned the pilgrim stand up as well.<br />
Leading to the oak’s trunk broad embrace,<br />
He placed a hand on bark, knotted and swell.<br />
“This oak has lived through ages untold,<br />
Burned by lightning, by drought nearly fell;<br />
Yet each scar deepened wisdom it could hold.<br />
In its heartwood, a hollow chamber rings—<br />
The tree is empty, thus grows more bold.<br />
So must you become, an empty offering:<br />
Hollow out the last vestige of ego’s claim,<br />
Then shall your soul like this tree truly sing.”

He knocked on the trunk; a resonant frame<br />
Sounded within, echo pure and deep.<br />
“Enter the hollow, if thou seeketh aim<br />
To finally drink from truth’s fountain steep.”<br />
The pilgrim noticed now an opening<br />
Among roots—a doorway where one could creep.<br />
He hesitated a moment, heart fluttering,<br />
Then stooped and entered that wooden cave.<br />
Inside, dim light filtered, softly uttering<br />
Through cracks, and the scent of oak so gave<br />
Comfort akin to incense at prayer.<br />
The hollow trunk was snug as a grave.

Surprisingly, within there was a stair<br />
Carved winding upward, narrow and old.<br />
The sage’s voice floated, “Ascend if you dare.”<br />
With courage steadying, he took hold<br />
Of twisting staircase inside the tree’s core,<br />
Climbing upward through darkness uncontrolled.<br />
The way felt endless, circling more and more,<br />
As if ascending to another world.<br />
His limbs grew heavy and doubt as before.<br />
But now he recalled how illusions swirled<br />
When nearing truth; likely one final test—<br />
To discourage the soul with fear unfurled.

So onward he climbed, resolved and blessed,<br />
Reciting quietly prayers from each faith<br />
He’d learned, keeping heart and mind at rest.<br />
At last, a glimmer above like wraith<br />
Showed an exit from that wooden tunnel.<br />
He emerged on a ledge, catching breath’s lathe.<br />
To his astonishment, beyond that funnel<br />
Stretched not the meadow or sky he’d left,<br />
But an infinite space—stars without kennel.<br />
He seemed afloat in infinity, bereft<br />
Of the mountain, the oak, of earth below;<br />
The ledge he stood on hung in space, a cleft

In existence itself, where dreams grow<br />
Before him, an apparition took form—<br />
A being of light no shadow could throw.<br />
It wore the likeness of all he’d seen warm:<br />
The hermit, the hooded guide, the Sufi,<br />
The elder, the sage—faces shift in swarm.<br />
At times it was beloved friends in duty,<br />
At times unknown but radiantly fair,<br />
Male, female, and androgynous in beauty.<br />
Finally, it settled to one face rare—<br />
The pilgrim’s own face, but crowned with fire,<br />
Eyes full of love, free of burden or care.

This self-not-self smiled and rose up higher,<br />
Standing upon nothing, yet firm and real.<br />
It spoke without sound, but clear as choir<br />
In the pilgrim’s mind, making true appeal:<br />
“You have journeyed far, dear self of mine,<br />
Shed blood and tears in pursuit of the ideal.<br />
Know now that I am the one you pine<br />
To meet, yet I’ve been with you all along.<br />
I am the timeless soul in you, divine.<br />
You carved God from hunger—this was not wrong,<br />
For it led you onward, step after step,<br />
Each idol shattered when proved empty song.

Your hunger, like fire, burned dross and kept<br />
You seeking purer to quench the desire,<br />
Til at last through illusions you have leapt.<br />
Now standing at edge of truth’s utmost spire,<br />
Only one veil remains: the seeker’s pride,<br />
The sense of separate self you must retire.”<br />
The pilgrim trembled, though not to deride<br />
The words—in them resonated fate’s tone.<br />
Yet what was being asked, to cast aside<br />
His self entirely? would nothing be known?<br />
Sensing fear, the radiant image stepped close,<br />
Placed light-hand on heart, dispelling the lone.

In that touch, lifetimes of hurt arose,<br />
Regrets, attachments, all hopes and all fears,<br />
Flashed before his eyes in mournful prose.<br />
He saw childhood loneliness, youth’s lost years,<br />
Faces of those he’d loved and lost to time,<br />
Achievements and failures that drew out tears.<br />
He saw the one moment sublime<br />
When he first glimpsed the Beloved within<br />
During the song in the temple’s prime.<br />
All these he held dear, his narrative skin,<br />
The story that he called “me” and “my own.”<br />
To surrender this—did liberation begin?

“Will I vanish?” he asked in trembling tone.<br />
The presence answered, “Only what is false.<br />
Your true self divine can never be gone.<br />
Like drop in ocean, your small ego falls<br />
Into larger being—ceases as drop,<br />
Yet as oceanic oneness enthralls.<br />
You will still act, think, love, but shall adopt<br />
The perspective of greater unity.<br />
No longer by fears of ego be stopped.”<br />
The pilgrim-soul considered destiny,<br />
Remembering the pain of separate life,<br />
The glimpses of joy in community.

He thought of the valley of trials rife,<br />
People he helped, the moments of pure grace,<br />
When he forgot himself in healing strife.<br />
Those times felt meaningful beyond replace.<br />
Perhaps this is the secret of saints long sung—<br />
To lose oneself in all, emptiness embrace.<br />
Steeling resolve from what bell had rung,<br />
He nodded slowly, ready to release,<br />
Trusting that love would catch him when un-sprung.<br />
The radiant self then gave him peace:<br />
“I am what happens when you set aside<br />
The hunger that carves false gods, and find peace.”

At that, the starry void opened wide<br />
Behind the figure, revealing a gate<br />
Formed of light in filigree undenied.<br />
It beckoned gently, as if to negate<br />
Any final doubt with allure of truth.<br />
The pilgrim stepped forward, embracing fate.<br />
At the threshold he paused, a moment sooth,<br />
Looked back one last time at what was “I”,<br />
Then with a breath stepped through as new youth.<br />
In blinding flash he felt himself untie—<br />
No, dissolve—no, expand—words cannot say,<br />
A drop returning to sea with soft sigh.

He felt all boundaries melting away,<br />
Sense of self diffused in existence entire.<br />
He was the sunlight dancing on the bay,<br />
And the bay itself, deep, living sapphire.<br />
He was fusion, and hydrogen, and helium,<br />
And the dark between, infinite quire.<br />
He was the laughter of children certain,<br />
And the tears of the grieving mother too.<br />
He was the questing sage and the urchin.<br />
He was wind on grass and the morning dew,<br />
All at once in one vast awareness merged—<br />
An “I” as big as the All, fresh and new.

There was no pilgrim left that could be urged,<br />
Yet paradoxically he remained true—<br />
A presence through which universe now surged.<br />
How long he stayed in that union through<br />
Beyond time’s meaning, cannot be measured,<br />
Could be an instant or eons in lieu.<br />
In mortal world, a flash of light treasured<br />
Burst from the oak’s hollow and kissed the sky,<br />
A meteor by which night was pleasured.<br />
The sage at foot of tree beheld it fly,<br />
Smile knowingly at the sign of success,<br />
Then closed his eyes with a contented sigh.
