Nightfall approaches in the woods,<br />
A wanderer stands with heart atremble bare,<br />
An exile lost where shadow’s secrets lie.<br />
Forgotten path dim-lit by hope and prayer,<br />
He drifts ‘twixt cedars old as mortal sin;<br />
Their boughs like elders in their silent stare.<br />
The voice of dusk bid his quest begin,<br />
As leaves in vespers sway with sigh and groan—<br />
He wakes from world’s illusions thick and thin.<br />
In solitude, behold: he stands alone,<br />
While dying sunbeams bleed in threads of gold,<br />
His spirit famished, soul an empty throne.

Footfalls unsteady on a path untold,<br />
He staggers deeper into yawning gloom,<br />
His hunger carving out what hearts can’t hold.<br />
The forest hums a half-forgotten tune,<br />
Chant of the ancients from a distant space,<br />
Where Plato’s Forms in silent vigil loom:<br />
The True and Good beyond our earthly helm,<br />
A world of patterns, primal and ideal,<br />
Reflecting wisdom that mere flesh o’erwhelms.<br />
This pilgrim yearns for truths no dream can steal,<br />
But each step falters on the needful stone;<br />
His mortal doubts around his ankles congeal.

The path like serpent coils ‘neath mossy bone,<br />
No straight road clear to promised shining gate;<br />
Thus mortal mind by paradox is thrown.<br />
An ashen fox darts past in frantic state,<br />
A fleeting spectre ‘cross the pilgrim’s track;<br />
Its feral eyes agleam with cunning fate.<br />
That ember gaze which glows in fur of black<br />
Seers through his essence to the hunger fraught,<br />
As if to judge the burden on his back.<br />
Our seeker shudders, conscience keenly caught,<br />
By spectres of his sins that cease to rest,<br />
Old idols carved from yearnings we have wrought.

In hush of eve the wood becomes confessor,<br />
An altar carved of oak and thirst and flame,<br />
His memories laid bare as by possessor.<br />
Each trunk a pillar in this psychic frame,<br />
Each root an anchor dragging down to truth—<br />
He wades through recollection bound by shame.<br />
There in the thicket, voice of quiet sooth<br />
Calls from the cedar’s heart in patient tone,<br />
An elder spirit speaking to lost youth:<br />
“O pilgrim, weary treading all alone,<br />
What draws thee through this labyrinth of dread?<br />
What thirst compels thy soul from hearth and home?”

The wanderer, startled, lifts his heavy head,<br />
He scans the gloom for who addressed him thus<br />
And spies an ancient hermit robed in red.<br />
Bent by weight of years, beard silvery muss,<br />
This figure smiles with eyes of ageless grace,<br />
A gentle solace in the night’s discuss.<br />
He leans upon a staff of knotted lace,<br />
Each twist of wood carved with an olden tongue,<br />
Symbols of star and sun along its face.<br />
His voice like leaves that withering have sung,<br />
Yet strong enough to shake the cypress old:<br />
“Seek ye in darkness what from light hath sprung?

Long have I waited in this midnight cold<br />
For one whose hunger leads him to this gate,<br />
To test thy heart like iron in the mold.<br />
Heed now the path which secret tongues relate,<br />
For this dark forest hides the only door<br />
Into the truth beyond mere mortal fate.<br />
Though worn and lost, thy soul holds ancient lore;<br />
Within thee lies the spark of holy flame,<br />
To light the way where none have walked before.<br />
Fear not the shadow nor thy errant shame—<br />
Each step in darkness sets the spirit free.<br />
Thy hunger carves a god—here endeth game.”

The wanderer’s knees quiver suddenly,<br />
As though the earth itself had softly sighed;<br />
He bows before wisdom on bended knee.<br />
“Who art thou?” to the hermit he replied,<br />
Voice hoarse with thirst for counsel and for grace;<br />
“My spirit starves, by hollow dreams denied.<br />
In sooth, I come from life’s fruitless chase,<br />
Where every promise turned to dust and wind.<br />
No idol sates, no mortal wealth can place<br />
Contentment in my heart nor peace of mind.<br />
I yearn to grasp what ancient seers have known,<br />
A truth eternal none can break or bind.”

The hermit nods, as if these words were grown<br />
From seeds he planted eons past in time.<br />
“Thy thirst,” quoth he, “no less than mine hath shown—<br />
For mortal souls must rise through dirt and grime<br />
To sip the dew that hides on heaven’s bough.<br />
In hunger’s maw, desire becomes sublime.<br />
Mark well the path: what matters is the how,<br />
Not what illusions promise to provide.<br />
To carve a god from hunger shapes a vow,<br />
Yet knows not how to fill the void inside.<br />
Step forth, my child, the journey must commence<br />
Through rites of old where truth and self collide.”

So saying, he extends a hand immense,<br />
A bony limb with strength of oaken age;<br />
Our pilgrim clasps it, trusting in his sense.<br />
With step in stride they breach the forest’s cage,<br />
Where roots retreat and gloom concedes to night—<br />
Each bough bent back to carve a narrow stage.<br />
The wanderer follows, heart drumming contrite,<br />
As though ascending some primordial stair<br />
Where mind transcends this worldly fleshly plight.<br />
The hermit leads, a flame beyond despair,<br />
His staff aglow with runes of hidden light,<br />
Illuming brambles in soft saffron glare.

In winding serpentines they climb through night,<br />
Past skulls of fallen trees and beasts decayed;<br />
In deathly ruin flickers insight’s sight.<br />
Our seeker, in each trunk with rot arrayed,<br />
Sees mirrored glimpses of himself undone,<br />
As if his vice in wood and worm displayed.<br />
Here lust, there pride, envy beneath the sun,<br />
Carven in bark or crawling in the loam;<br />
Ghosts of the soul transmuted one by one.<br />
With each foul visage seen the more to roam,<br />
He feels a shedding of some heavy weight,<br />
A scale from ego peeled, another comb.

Thus purged by vision of each dire strait<br />
His faltering heart had sailed by folly’s gale,<br />
He feels new breath beyond his mortal state.<br />
At last, they reach a clearing hushed and pale,<br />
A glade where moonlight paints a silver dome,<br />
An argent sea wherein ideas set sail.<br />
The hermit stops beside a granite home<br />
Of rugged stone with moss in ancient script,<br />
Its surface carved with creatures that do roam.<br />
This monolith in shimmering light is dripped,<br />
Pale sentinel of secrets to unfold;<br />
In each strange glyph a prophecy is crypt.

Upon its face the hermit’s staff takes hold,<br />
His withered fingers trace a symbol raw—<br />
A triangle entwined with circle bold.<br />
At once, the stone begins to quake with awe,<br />
A moaning creak as doorway appears,<br />
Inviting them through mouth of ancient maw.<br />
With gracious nod, the sage allays his fears:<br />
“Behold the threshold to the deeper wood—<br />
There lies the temple of the hidden seers.<br />
What must be gleaned beyond is understood<br />
Not through the eyes alone but by the heart,<br />
And by the hunger that would carve a god.”

Thus beckoned, pilgrim readies to depart<br />
This mortal glade for fields of mythic truth.<br />
Though fear yet coils, resolve withstands its smart.<br />
Into the darkness carved by ageless youth—<br />
The youthful hunger old as humankind—<br />
He steps behind his guide in twilight’s ruth.<br />
Stone lips seal shut as soon as they enshrined<br />
Their passing forms, entombing them in night.<br />
No way but onward through the cavern blind.<br />
Though unaccustomed to this lack of sight,<br />
He feels the hermit’s presence like a flame,<br />
Each footfall guided by that ghostly light.

Their path descends through corridors that claim<br />
The echoes of their steps in solemn halls,<br />
Where unseen choirs whisper secret Name.<br />
A spectral breeze through emptiness now calls,<br />
Carrying chants of distant devotees,<br />
In languages ere Babel’s tower falls.<br />
The wanderer’s heart leaps—could one of these<br />
Be those who from all climes sought wisdom high?<br />
Now disembodied, swirling as the seas?<br />
He whispers soft: “What voices here comply?<br />
Are these illusions wrought by weary mind,<br />
Or counsel’s heralds drawn from space on high?”

The hermit’s voice returns both calm and kind:<br />
“Each echo here is memory of prayer,<br />
A plea by pilgrims past now so entwined.<br />
They sought in darkness paths beyond despair,<br />
Carving their longing out of flesh and thought,<br />
Invoking truth beyond what senses share.<br />
Their words yet linger—lessons dearly bought—<br />
To guide new seekers at the journey’s start.<br />
Heed well their ghostly choir where hope is wrought.”<br />
As if on cue, one voice from void apart<br />
Rings clearly in that moment through the air:<br />
“A fire is kindled by friction of heart with heart.”

The wanderer clings to the hermit’s care,<br />
While stone beneath their feet slopes further down;<br />
The darkness thickens, heavy to compare.<br />
The guide’s soft light in gloom a spectral crown,<br />
Glinting on damp walls carved with mystic art—<br />
In runes unknown the truth of yore is sown.<br />
At length, a welcome glow begins to start<br />
Ahead, an ember, like a lone star set<br />
In midnight well beyond the world apart.<br />
Their footsteps quicken, hope and caution met,<br />
For at that flame an altar altar stands,<br />
A vigil left in cavern’s cryptic net.

They near and see, with eyes and heart and hands,<br />
A lamp of ancient oil sputtering low,<br />
Upon an altar carved in these far lands.<br />
Each carving on its face speaks what we know<br />
And what we yearn yet still to comprehend—<br />
In swirling patterns, flames and trees do flow.<br />
In one corner a phoenix meets its end,<br />
In raging fire to ashes cold and grey,<br />
Yet from that ash climbs newborn to ascend.<br />
Another side: an oak stands in decay,<br />
Hollowed by age and struck by lightning white,<br />
But from charred stump shoots green to find its day.

And there, inscribed with gentlest of delight,<br />
A humble carpenter at work with wood<br />
Carves forms of beauty birthed from inner sight.<br />
Our wanderer beholds and understood<br />
The motif weaving through these varied scenes:<br />
Of transformation wrought from bad or good.<br />
Fire, wood, carving—visions each convenes<br />
To whisper something just beyond his grasp,<br />
Like half-forgotten tales from childhood dreams.<br />
His fingers trace the woodcarver’s firm clasp<br />
Upon the chisel shaping holy face,<br />
Perhaps a god drawn out with each light rasp.

Then out from darkness steps in sudden grace<br />
A new arrival robed in raven black,<br />
A hood concealing visage from this place.<br />
Though silent footfalls gave no hint or track,<br />
Our wanderer senses presence now profound,<br />
A soul awakened from time’s endless slack.<br />
The hermit bows to one on sacred ground,<br />
A greeting given not with word but heart,<br />
As if two kin by destiny are bound.<br />
The hooded one then draws a bit apart,<br />
Unveiling ancient eyes both young and old,<br />
Within whose depths universes impart.

The pilgrim trembles though not from the cold—<br />
For recognition stirs in marrow deep,<br />
As if those eyes his own essence behold.<br />
A voice ensues from caverns half-asleep,<br />
Gentle and fierce all in one measured note:<br />
“Brave soul who dares through midnight gloom to creep,<br />
What is it that you seek in worlds remote?”<br />
The wanderer finds his voice with trembling sound:<br />
“I seek the Truth beyond life’s antidote.<br />
I seek to quench the thirst where none abound,<br />
To know what meaning fires thee eternal wheel,<br />
What hidden patterns in our fate are wound.”

The hooded figure nods with stern appeal,<br />
And in those eyes a flame begins to dance,<br />
A flicker born from fervor’s holy zeal.<br />
“To hunger for the Truth is no mere chance,”<br />
Intones the figure, raising slender hand,<br />
“But mortal hearts can starve from ignorance.<br />
To carve a god from hunger—understand—<br />
Is to risk worship of a hollow form,<br />
A phantom by desires falsely manned.<br />
Thus path to wisdom first must bravely storm<br />
Each vain illusion to the very core,<br />
And break the idols by true vision shorn.”

Our wanderer hears and feels an ache he bore<br />
Since earliest youth: the fear of being blind<br />
To life’s grand meaning, lost forevermore.<br />
He sees again those idols in the mind<br />
That one by one in forest he had glimpsed—<br />
Their promises false joys that ever bind.<br />
In rising sorrow at a life misspent,<br />
He bows his head, choked by a voiceless cry;<br />
Two glistening tears from laden eyelids vent.<br />
They drop upon the dusty stone to die,<br />
Dark patches on the temple’s ancient floor—<br />
As though Earth drinks the offering from sky.

The hooded sage extends a hand once more<br />
And lifts the pilgrim’s chin with gentle grace,<br />
“Your tears anoint the path we seek and soar.<br />
Within your grief lies gift to here unlace,<br />
For emptiness can hold the seed of all—<br />
In void of sorrow sprouts joy’s sacred face.<br />
Now steel your spirit for what shall befall,<br />
And trust the fire that purifies the soul;<br />
We journey forth through zones beyond the wall.”<br />
At that, the hermit and this sage cajole<br />
The wanderer toward a further gate,<br />
A threshold leading to the second scroll.

The pilgrim stepping through with trembling feet,<br />
His old life cast aside, stripped desolate.<br />
In darkness borne by guiding lights he meets<br />
A flicker of hope where wisdom’s flame entreats.
