## Chapter Promise
Embody courage without propaganda. She is not a poster. She is a person who should not have had to become a weapon.

## Draft Frame
Final prose target: 1,100 words.

## Scene Architecture
Outside the city, I see young women at anti-aircraft guns firing at tanks. Their training is inadequate; their courage is not. One girl has a school ribbon tied inside her sleeve.

## Key Narrative Beats
- I approach carrying ammunition.
- She tells me to stay low.
- She fires at a tank; the recoil knocks her shoulder.
- She says she was supposed to become a mathematics teacher.
- A plane dives.
- She does not run.
- The gun keeps firing after I stop hearing anything else.

## Narrator Movement
I stop merely witnessing and assist. I carry shells. I become implicated.

## Transition
The gun sight becomes a school compass drawing circles.
