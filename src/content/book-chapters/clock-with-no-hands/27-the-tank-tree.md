## Chapter Promise
The central redemptive image. Technology is not purified by forgetting its violence; it is converted by human purpose.

## Draft Frame
Final prose target: 1,300 words.

## Scene Architecture
The burned tank from Stalingrad rolls into the court. Its barrel points at the crowd. Everyone freezes.

Then the girl from the anti-aircraft gun steps forward and places her hand on its armor. I place mine beside hers. Others follow.

The tank changes.

## Transformations
- Barrel fills with soil.
- Engine becomes a hospital pump.
- Armor plates become shelter roofs.
- Radio becomes a classroom transmitter.
- Treads become irrigation belts.
- Targeting optics become telescopes.
- Ammunition racks become seed drawers.
- A tree grows from the turret. Its leaves are metallic at first, then green.

## Craft Constraint
Do not make this sentimental. The dead remain dead. The tree grows from grief, not innocence.

## Transition
One fruit falls from the tree. It is a small sun. When it hits the floor, the clock returns.
