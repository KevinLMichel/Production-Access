## Chapter Promise
Introduce fatalism and the possibility of steering.

## Draft Frame
Final prose target: 1,000 words.

## Scene Architecture
I am on a ship crossing a dark ocean. Behind it is a luminous wake containing images: wars, childhoods, strikes, empires, debts, revolutions, family photographs, court documents, factory gates, bomb craters.

Everyone aboard faces backward. They argue over the foam. Some blame the foam for the ship’s direction. Some worship it. Some say nothing can be done because the wake has already chosen.

I turn toward the bow. There is no captain.

## Key Narrative Beats
- I watch passengers debate the wake as if it were steering.
- I see my own footprints in the foam, impossible but visible.
- A crewman tells me no one has checked the wheel in years.
- I go to the helm.
- The wheel is tied down with ribbons labeled “tradition,” “necessity,” “market,” “security,” “destiny.”
- I do not untie them yet. I only touch them.

## Transition
The ship horn becomes a factory whistle.
