## Scene Architecture
I am back inside the clock. The cities remain around the rim. They are still burning, building, teaching, producing, consuming. The clock still has no hands.

But now I am different.

## Key Narrative Beats
- I walk to the center.
- The ribbons around the ship’s wheel are there too, tied around the clock’s central pin.
- Tradition. Necessity. Market. Security. Destiny.
- I untie them one by one.
- Each ribbon becomes a road leading outward.
- The snake appears, watching.
- The child from the classroom asks what time it is.
- I look at my wounded palm.
- I raise my hand toward the clock face.

## Transition
My shadow falls across the clock face like a hand.
