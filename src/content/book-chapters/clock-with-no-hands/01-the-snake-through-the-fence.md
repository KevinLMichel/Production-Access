## Chapter Promise
Introduce the idea that events are artificially separated, but only through action: I must move, then break the plank.

## Draft Frame
Final prose target: 950 words.

## Scene Architecture
A fence appears across the clock face. I look through a narrow slit and see a snake pass: head first, then body, then tail. I assume the head summons the tail. Then the snake circles back. Again head, body, tail.

A child beside me says, “The head makes the tail happen.”

I almost agree. Then I step sideways, away from the slit, and see the whole snake at once. It is not cause and effect. It is one body.

## Key Narrative Beats
- I realize the fence creates false sequence.
- The child insists the slit is “how educated people look.”
- I break one plank from the fence.
- More of the snake becomes visible.
- The snake coils into a ring.
- Its scales become clock teeth.

## Narrator Movement
First small act of agency: I alter the viewing frame.

## Transition
The broken plank floats away like driftwood on black water.
