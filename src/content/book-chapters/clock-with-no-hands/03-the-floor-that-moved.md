## Chapter Promise
Make alienation physical before naming nothing.

## Draft Frame
Final prose target: 1,100 words.

## Scene Architecture
I stand on a factory floor that extends beyond sight. It contains textile looms, car assembly lines, hospital billing offices, server farms, slaughterhouses, drone plants, fulfillment warehouses, classrooms, and military laboratories. The machinery is mismatched but synchronized.

No one asks what is being made.

## Key Narrative Beats
- I follow a conveyor belt carrying shoes, rifles, diplomas, insulin vials, phones, eviction notices, and medals.
- Workers perform narrow tasks without seeing completed products.
- A man tightens bolts on hospital beds he cannot afford to lie in.
- A woman labels medicine she cannot buy.
- A coder writes instructions for software that monitors his productivity.
- A child sweeps metal shavings under a sign reading: OPPORTUNITY.

## Narrator Movement
I begin asking workers what they make. None can answer fully.

## Transition
The conveyor belt rises into the air and enters a glass office.
