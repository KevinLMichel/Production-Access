## Chapter Promise
The river becomes the moral and temporal center.

## Draft Frame
Final prose target: 1,100 words.

## Scene Architecture
The Volga is both river and border. Boats carry wounded east and soldiers west. Ammunition goes one way; blood returns the other.

## Key Narrative Beats
- I try to wash soot from my hands in burning water.
- A boatman laughs without humor.
- Soldiers waiting to cross stare at the burning skyline.
- One asks me if there is ground beyond the river.
- Before I answer, another says, “Not for us.”
- A wounded man begs not for rescue but for tobacco.
- A woman searches for her mother among bodies on the bank.

## Narrator Movement
I begin helping carry wounded, not because I understand the world but because in front of suffering the first moral act is physical.

## Transition
Shells fall near the bank. I dive into a drainage tunnel.
