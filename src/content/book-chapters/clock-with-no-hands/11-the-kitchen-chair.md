## Chapter Promise
Show civilian life repurposed into war machinery.

## Draft Frame
Final prose target: 950 words.

## Scene Architecture
I sit on the kitchen chair in a blown-out upper room. An artillery observer uses a periscope through a shell hole. He calls coordinates to guns across the river.

## Key Narrative Beats
- The chair still has carved initials from a family.
- The observer is polite, almost scholarly.
- He asks me to hold the field telephone wire.
- I do.
- When the guns fire from the far bank, the whole room shakes.
- The observer says, “There. A battalion will not arrive now.”
- I look at the chair and imagine breakfast.

## Transition
The phone wire coils into a snake and slides under a classroom door.
