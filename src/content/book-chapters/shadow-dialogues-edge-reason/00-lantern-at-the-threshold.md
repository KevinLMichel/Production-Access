Before forbidden gates the Pilgrim stands,<br />
A city wrought of paradox and shade;<br />
A lantern shakes within his trembling hands.

Its flame, a soul by silent anguish made,<br />
Flickers against the depthless shroud of black,<br />
Where whispered truths in scented winds cascade.

The air is laced with promise and attack—<br />
A perfume rich with dreams that dare not die,<br />
Yet fouled by secrets trailing sorrow’s track.

Each breath he takes is drawn with weary sigh,<br />
Metallic with betrayal’s ghosted taste—<br />
A memory that lingers, leech and lie.

The wound he bears is veiled, yet never placed<br />
Beyond the pulse of grief that shapes his path;<br />
Exiled, he roams through silence deep and waste.

By faithless love undone, by judgment's wrath,<br />
Through ash and night and sunless sands he came,<br />
Till now he halts beneath the city’s math.

He lifts his light—no shield, no boast, no flame—<br />
But still it shines on stones that speak no name.

This is the dream where myths in silence speak,<br />
A city sung in tales that fade with time—<br />
Where soul and shadow share their mirrored cheek.

Its spires, like serpents, climb in curves sublime<br />
To pierce the firmament, now void of light;<br />
Its streets, like thoughts, in spiral sleep align.

No sentry stands to ward the dreamer’s flight,<br />
No hearth - glow greets the stranger’s wary eyes—<br />
Just open archways swallowing the night.

The Pilgrim feels his heart in dread arise,<br />
Yet bound with longing deep as sacred fire;<br />
He knows not if this gate be curse or prize.

The past behind is sealed, no more entire,<br />
And so he stands, not free—but forward drawn—<br />
His lamp a flicker kindled from the mire.

Beneath his feet, the runes are carved and wan,<br />
A script unknown—perhaps a welcome's dawn,<br />
Perhaps a ward where ancient danger’s pawn.

He steps within—the stone beneath resounds,<br />
As if a cave had stirred to sudden breath;<br />
The city's dark, attentive, wraps all bounds.

This dark is not mere void, nor passive death,<br />
But lives, it watches, pulses with his tread,<br />
And shares his breath as one who waits beneath.

He lifts the lantern higher o’er his head;<br />
Its meager flame drives back a trembling span,<br />
Yet leaves the greater gloom untouched, unread.

The silence speaks of watchers not yet man,<br />
Of eyes that blink in walls too old to fall,<br />
Of minds that wait beyond time’s measured plan.

Four roads unfurl before him in the hall,<br />
Each veiled in shadow’s robe, each void of sign;<br />
Yet in his chest he feels a mystic call.

No chart is drawn, no compass may align,<br />
But fate, or something kindred, gives him spine,<br />
And draws him forth through thresholds serpentine.

He halts once more upon the threshold's line,<br />
And in that pause, the wound within does flare,<br />
A pulse of pain, the echo of design.

He sees again the smile, the sudden glare,<br />
The hand once trusted, now the viper's kin;<br />
Was it a friend—or love—that wrought despair?

The moment strikes him like a frost within,<br />
A fleeting lash across his guarded chest,<br />
Where memory and sorrow intertwine.

How sweet the seed of bitterness could rest,<br />
And take its root where soil of grief is deep;<br />
Yet he denies it, though his heart protests.

He closes eyes as one who dares to leap,<br />
And breathes, a breath he held through shadowed years,<br />
And in its hush, a vow he dares to keep.

No god of old receives his silent tears,<br />
But night itself, and all the unknown ways,<br />
He prays to now, and casts aside his fears.

“Let truth guide me,” he whispers, voice barely more than a thought. “Let suffering turn to insight, let this exile become a path.” The lantern flickers as if in answer.

Thus does he pass into the city’s keep,<br />
Where silence rises like a vaulted hymn,<br />
A shadowed choir where light forgets to sleep.

Through half - seen paths and arches ghostly dim,<br />
The dream unfolds its labyrinthine grace,<br />
And breathes a hush both reverent and grim.

The air is changed—cool, steeped in sacred space,<br />
As though the walls themselves draw in a breath;<br />
A stillness clothed in time’s forgotten face.

A murmur stirs, though none give voice to death,<br />
Yet on the edge of sense, soft whispers play—<br />
Or is it blood that drums its mortal meth?

He hears not words, but winds that drift and sway:<br />
Power, pleasure, silence, truth they name,<br />
Unspoken heralds veiled in twilight’s gray.

He knows them not, nor yet their trials’ claim,<br />
But feels their gaze, like watchers on a stage,<br />
Where he, unbidden, walks into their frame.

With lantern high, the Pilgrim takes his stride,<br />
His solitude a cloak both worn and thin;<br />
Yet ghostly kin walk silent at his side.

His memories, like phantoms drawn within,<br />
And longing, like a shadow's gentle breath,<br />
Accompany his path where none have been.

The wound remains, though not the wound of death,<br />
But that which mends through motion’s solemn art;<br />
Each step a stitch that hems the seam of wrath.

Behind, the gate dissolves, a dream’s depart;<br />
Before him, broader winds the widening way,<br />
Where silence speaks to courage in the heart.

No stars above the dream - city hold sway,<br />
Only the swirl of dark that reigns the dome,<br />
Yet there a light flares faint as dawning day.

Upon a tower high or hidden home,<br />
It sparks, then others answer through the veil,<br />
Like whispered names of lords who never roam.

He sees not all, yet each begins the tale:<br />
A citadel for Power, proud and high;<br />
A garden glows where Pleasure's masks prevail;

Truth waits in mirrors none may yet descry,<br />
While Silence guards the deep in cloistered gloom—<br />
And each now marks his passage with their eye.

Now through the narrow street his path is wound,<br />
Where stones, time - slick, breathe sweat from dreaming stone,<br />
As though the city sleeps with pulse and sound.

He feels within, not wholly not alone,<br />
A dream not forged by fate or fate alone,<br />
But shaped by self, by wound, by loss well known.

No path remains to lead the Pilgrim home,<br />
What’s left is forward, through what trials wait,<br />
Through veils of dread and voices carved in loam.

He draws a breath to quell the weight of fate,<br />
Lets silence hold him like a mother’s shroud,<br />
And pulls his cloak against the soul’s cold state.

The chill he fights is one no wind has ploughed,<br />
But rises from within, where sorrows steep;<br />
Yet still he walks, though pain within him howled.

The lantern casts its light in circles deep,<br />
Where motes like trembling stars begin to glide,<br />
Small worlds that twirl where quiet shadows sleep.

He watches them and sees, though mystified,<br />
That even lights so frail can shift the gloom,<br />
That darkness parts when lesser flames abide.

He was a flicker at the city’s womb,<br />
A flame near spent, near silence, near eclipse;<br />
But now he steps beyond the gate of doom.

And though no god has kissed his mortal lips,<br />
He bears the hope that through these dream - born trips,<br />
He’ll learn to ask the truth with steady grips.
