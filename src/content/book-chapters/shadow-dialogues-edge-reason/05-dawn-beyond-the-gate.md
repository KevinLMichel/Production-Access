The Pilgrim stood where shadow meets the day,<br />
Where gates once dark now glow with morning's kiss;<br />
Behind, the city fades in light’s array.

Its towers rise in gilded, dreamlike bliss,<br />
Atriums bathed in gold, no longer grim—<br />
Yet feel as if from memory’s abyss.

The land he walked now dims along the brim<br />
Of waking thought, like mist that sun devours—<br />
Half dream, half scar, a song’s forgotten hymn.

Before him spreads the plain, wind - washed with hours<br />
Of dew and green, where sunrise strokes the land,<br />
And breath of hope returns in tender powers.

The breeze flows through the gate with beckoning hand,<br />
And tugs his cloak as though to softly say:<br />
"Come, let your heart where light begins now stand."

He stepped beyond, where boundless grasses gleam,<br />
And left the city's breathless gate behind;<br />
Its hush, not prison, but release from dream.

The doors swung closed with quiet, measured mind,<br />
Yet not in wrath, nor in forgetful spite—<br />
But like a book, once read, once underlined.

A dozen paces on, atop a height,<br />
He turned to glimpse the walls, now still and grave;<br />
The sun caught glass and sparked a farewell light.

It flared—a final star, a gift it gave<br />
From Reason's halls where voices once had tried<br />
To shape his soul through trial, truth, and wave.

He bowed his head, with silence for his guide,<br />
And turned once more to meet the living field,<br />
With all he'd gained now kindled deep inside.

No longer bound, yet nothing left concealed—<br />
For Pleasure, Power, Silence, Truth remain<br />
As threads within the self they sought to wield.

Before him stretched the vast and breathing plain,<br />
Where wildflowers bright and secret paths unfold;<br />
No souls in sight, and yet he feels no pain.

The sky above was blue and bright and bold,<br />
And larks in song stitched morning into air,<br />
While sunlight danced on footprints damp and cold.

Each step he took through grass still wet and fair<br />
Left glistening marks, like runes upon the earth,<br />
Then vanished in the heat with gentle care.

So walks the one reborn by shadowed birth—<br />
A flame now carried forward, shaped by worth.

Still in his hand, the lantern’s weight he bore—<br />
The brass grown dull, the glass with soot begrimed,<br />
Its frame now marred by trial’s weary chore.

Through fields of doubt its fragile fire had chimed,<br />
A ray through the veils of night and fear,<br />
Yet now, beneath the sun’s full face, unprimed.

Within his chest, a clearer flame burned clear,<br />
One kindled not by wax, but woven thought—<br />
A light no glass could hold, no oil endear.

He knelt where grasses kissed the earth and caught<br />
The morning’s breath, and set the lantern low,<br />
Its task complete, its final journey brought.

The stub of candle trembled with a glow—<br />
A tongue of fire, slender as whispered grace,<br />
That danced against the wind, too weak to grow.

He opened up the door, and from its place<br />
Removed the flame, and with a tender care<br />
Sought soil to give it rest, a burial space.

He scooped the earth, the ground still cool and fair,<br />
And laid the flame within the shallow bed,<br />
Then pressed it closed, releasing it to air.

A wisp of smoke rose twisting from the dead,<br />
Sweet - scented with the memory of light—<br />
A requiem of wax, a prayer unsaid.

He lingered there, one hand upon the night<br />
Now buried in the cradle of the land,<br />
And whispered soft: "May this spark birth the right."

His breath became the breeze, a wordless strand,<br />
And onward flowed, as fate and sky had planned.

He rose, and from the soil a stirring came—<br />
A gentle heave, as though the ground drew breath;<br />
The place he’d sown now swelled with secret flame.

The earth then broke, not with decay nor death,<br />
But with a sprout—a shoot both green and bright,<br />
That pushed toward heaven with a will beneath.

Its leaves unfurled, two tongues of golden light<br />
That caught the dawn and sang in silent grace,<br />
While roots below embraced the hidden night.

Before his eyes, it grew—a tree took place,<br />
Its silver bark now smooth, its form refined,<br />
Each leaf aglow with moon’s remembered face.

And at its crown, a bloom, by fate designed:<br />
A peach blossom, in blush and ivory dressed,<br />
Its petals trembling, fresh with dew and mind.

A laugh escaped the Pilgrim, deep and blessed,<br />
For now he knew—what once was torn and burned<br />
Had borne a gift from all he had confessed.

The peach from Pleasure’s bough, which once he turned<br />
Within his palm, now joined the lantern’s fire<br />
To birth a life where joy and thought had yearned.

He reached to touch a leaf with hushed desire,<br />
And felt it pulse—a heart within the tree,<br />
A sign that light and sweetness could conspire.

So let it grow, for future souls to see—<br />
A fruit of wisdom kissed by revelry.

He might have stayed to tend the blooming tree,<br />
To watch its leaves catch light and grow in grace,<br />
But greater calls drew onward, wild and free.

He took the lantern—empty now, its face<br />
Still black with soot, its fire long since flown—<br />
And hung it at his side, a sacred trace.

It bore the weight of nights he’d walked alone,<br />
Yet now it swung with quiet, hollow pride,<br />
A relic from the dark that he had known.

The sun had risen, banished morning’s tide<br />
Of mist from off the plain, and gold did spill<br />
Upon the path that stretched both broad and wide.

He walked with ease, his steps without the chill<br />
Of yesterday, his pace no longer chained—<br />
Each stride a hymn of self, of voice, of will.

Before him stretched his shadow, long and plain,<br />
As though an old companion led the way,<br />
A guide that darkness once did entertain.

He climbed a gentle hill at break of day<br />
And paused to see the city far behind,<br />
Its towers faint through rising warmth and sway.

But eastward lay the world, unmarked, unlined—<br />
A realm of roads, of choices yet untried,<br />
Where winds of fate blew open paths unkind.

He breathed the air, where lark and breeze reside,<br />
Where grasses whispered with a tender tongue,<br />
And felt a peace that could no more divide.

For now he knew himself, both old and young—<br />
A soul once torn, now whole and bravely spun,<br />
Unshaken by the self he’d yet become.

He walked once more, beneath the waking sky,<br />
While far above, a flock of birds took wing,<br />
Their arcs like thoughts that dreamers let fly by.

He watched them turn toward dawn, and in their spring<br />
He felt his soul uplifted, light and whole,<br />
Unfettered now by sorrow’s tightening string.

The city’s trials, though etched upon his soul,<br />
Were not a chain but keys he bore with pride—<br />
And now life’s wider journey was his goal.

New joys would bloom, new griefs would not be denied;<br />
New powers called to test the strength he’d grown,<br />
New truths would burn, new silences abide.

But he, now tempered by what he had known,<br />
Could walk with care, and wield with heart aware—<br />
Could let go power, savor joy alone.

He bore within him courage bright and rare,<br />
To face what’s real, and see without disguise,<br />
And find the hush where wisdom takes its share.

And as his form grew small beneath wide skies,<br />
The tree he left, with blossom turned to gold,<br />
Stood glowing soft where shadow once did rise.

Perhaps another traveler, lost or bold,<br />
Would find that gate and there receive the shade—<br />
A fruit of peace from one who once was cold.

The breeze then stirred the grass as if it bade<br />
Him onward still, with whisper not of end,<br />
But of a song through morning’s hush replayed.

He did not turn—no glance did he extend;<br />
With silent step he joined the earth and dome,<br />
His form a thread the dawn would gently mend.

Yet vanish not—his tale still softly roams,<br />
Alive in wind, in lark, in lantern’s gleam;<br />
It lingers deep where seeking spirits roam.

If you but listen near the edge of dream,<br />
You’ll hear his steps, his voice, his vow, his flame—<br />
A Pilgrim’s song beneath the morning’s beam.

The gates are open—never closed the same.<br />
Each dawn invites the soul to cast its claim,<br />
To walk the path where silence sings your name.
