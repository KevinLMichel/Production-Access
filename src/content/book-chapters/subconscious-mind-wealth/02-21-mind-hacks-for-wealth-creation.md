Stream of Wealth Consciousness Writing

Set aside time once a week to write spontaneously about wealth creation. Before you begin, clear your conscious mind of any preconceived thoughts or ideas. Close your eyes and focus on your breathing for 20 seconds. As you pay attention to each breath, you’ll feel a sense of calm wash over you. When you're ready, quickly open your eyes and write for a minute or two.

Initially, this exercise may feel like a conscious effort, but with practice, you’ll begin to tap into your subconscious mind. Write freely about making and saving money without directing your thoughts toward a specific concept. Keep a log of your writings to track your progress and use for other exercises in this book.

After engaging in this “stream of wealth consciousness writing” exercise a dozen times or more, you’ll be able to harness this method to subconsciously write on a predetermined financial topic. Your writing will become a valuable tool for accessing your intuition and generating innovative financial strategies.

Before beginning the writing process, close your eyes, breathe deeply, and say to your subconscious: “I am about to write out a brilliant strategy for achieving xyz.” Then, awaken your senses and dive into the writing experience. By connecting with your subconscious mind, you'll unlock the potential for remarkable financial growth and success.

The Right Brain

Engage in any ‘right - brain’ creative pursuits to strengthen the connection between your conscious and subconscious mind. Art in its various forms, such as painting, cooking, filmmaking, drawing, stand - up comedy, poetry, storytelling, gardening, and countless other creative activities, can serve this purpose. Simply participating in these activities can connect you with your subconscious, as creative thinking enhances the link between the logical and imaginative parts of your brain. The subconscious is intrinsically connected to creative mental activity, and wealth creation demands a high level of creativity.

Understand The Brain

Expand your knowledge of the subconscious mind in psychology by reading books like “The Brain That Changes Itself” by Norman Doidge, M.D., or delving into the subject in another book called “Moving Through Parallel Worlds To Achieve Your Dreams.” Consume content on the subconscious mind, but focus on concepts that empower you. As the subconscious mind is infinite, seek material that demonstrates how to harness that power and disregard any that might limit your potential. If possible, enroll in a course on Positive Psychology and apply what you learn about the subconscious to your wealth creation goals.

Create Inception

Inception, in your context, represents the birth of a new idea, paradigm, or concept you're planting in your subconscious mind. Your mind is most receptive to inception just before falling asleep and immediately after waking up. During these moments, you have a brief, approximately 60 - second window to access your subconscious directly through autosuggestion. In the fleeting instant when you awaken and are still in a dream - like state, affirmations can bypass your conscious mind’s analytical filter. Your subconscious mind wholly believes what you tell it during these periods. If you encounter any negative responses to affirmations, it's your conscious mind speaking.

Every night before drifting off to sleep and each morning as you wake up, recite your financial goal with a definite completion date, and instruct your subconscious on how you want the day to unfold. Simultaneously, feel as if you've already accomplished this financial goal in the present moment.

Step 1: Read the goal with a specific completion date.

Step 2: Experience the emotion of having already achieved the goal.

To effectively implant ideas, be precise, state them positively, and infuse them with emotion, so your subconscious mind recognizes their importance amidst the myriad of random thoughts you have daily.

Nightly Audio

Be mindful of the audio you play while sleeping or opt for silence. (Ensure the chosen audio doesn’t disrupt your sleep, such as tracks with decibel spikes or overly engaging content.) Sounds in the background during sleep can permeate our dreams. Although your conscious mind might not perceive these sounds while you sleep, your subconscious mind registers many of them.

Be Alone

Every sentence uttered near you and each idea expressed by others imprints on your subconscious. To be alone offers a temporary respite from this barrage of external thoughts, allowing you the opportunity to hear your own inner voice. “The voice of the world will drown out the voice of God if you let it.” This doesn't mean you need to become an introvert unless it's your preference. Instead, simply carve out time to connect with yourself. In this context, the voice of ‘god’ represents your ‘richest self.’ The thoughts and words of those who struggle can cause you to struggle too, so find moments alone to concentrate on your financial greatness.

Be Still and Silent

At other times, seek a break from the clamor of your conscious mind. Choose moments where you intentionally do nothing and contemplate nothing. Admire a flower, walk along a trail, or spend time with a pet. Embrace some quiet time - put your phone on airplane mode, hibernate your computer, discard your television, and embrace silence. In this stillness, ideas for wealth creation will surface. Don't chase these ideas while in silent mode - simply note them briefly and return to the quiet.

“Let us be silent, that we may hear the whispers of the gods.” - Ralph Waldo Emerson

Be Focused

Directing your conscious mind toward a specific wealth - generating idea helps your subconscious grasp your intended goal. Maintain a clear, long - term financial objective to facilitate this communication. A narrow focus is crucial until you master the art of speaking to your subconscious. Within weeks, as you hone this skill, you'll be able to achieve, possess, and become more. Set a financial goal in any domain and remain committed to it for days, weeks, or months. If your conscious thoughts are scattered, your subconscious will return mixed results, uncertainty, and increased confusion - perpetuating a vicious cycle. Document and focus on specific monetary goals.

Ask Questions

Pose questions to your subconscious and let it uncover the answers. Instead of scouring the internet for something you once knew, ask your subconscious to retrieve the information. Be patient at first - it may even take a night’s sleep. As you practice, the process will accelerate until it’s virtually instantaneous, and your memory and strategic planning abilities will be remarkable. Use this same approach to seek your subconscious’s guidance in financial matters. Inquire about where to focus your investments of energy, time, and money.

Pursue A Passion

Engage in activities you love, whether it’s a sport, hobby, passion, or spending time with someone special. When you do something you love, your brain releases dopamine, which provides an almost spiritual energy. This feeling of spiritual connection flows from the subconscious to the conscious, fortifying the link between the two. Your brain forms physical connections between the unconscious neural structures and the prefrontal cortex (conscious mind). You don’t need to consciously guide this process for wealth creation - the goal is to temporarily let your mind become immersed in a world you're passionate about. The subconscious is constantly working, and it's most active when you’re in the flow of passion.

Create A Dream Log

Maintain a dream journal and contemplate their underlying messages. You can quickly jot down notes in the morning after waking up or start a habit of consciously analyzing your dreams while showering. The subconscious constantly seeks to connect with the conscious, just as you consciously attempt to connect with the subconscious. These two minds are meant to be partners. To nurture this partnership, you must listen to the subconscious mind just as you want it to listen to you. By briefly recording your dreams, you can gain insights into the challenges you face, find solutions to financial issues, and identify areas where you need to be more courageous.

Decoding your dreams requires practice, but as you become aware that the images are largely metaphorical and symbolic, your interpretation skills will improve. A dream journal encourages the subconscious to communicate with you, and the more you consciously listen to the subconscious, the more it will reciprocate. Asking the subconscious to reveal better communication methods will gradually yield results. It’s similar to asking a lover how to better understand and connect with them - while you may not receive an immediate response, continued attention will reveal hints, clues, and cues over time. Keeping a dream journal is a way to pay attention.

Listen to Your Intuition

Trust your instincts on small matters, even if they seem insignificant. Although you may be wrong in the early stages (unless the hunch concerns an area of expertise, in which case you might have an established link to your subconscious), the act of trusting hunches and gut feelings sends a message to your subconscious that you are listening and that this form of communication is welcome. Start with small decisions and gradually apply the technique to larger pursuits. In professional settings or while running your own business, you'll still need to provide logical explanations for your decisions, but continue practicing intuitive thinking. As you become a master of intuition, you’ll learn to rely on it to form hypotheses, test them, and develop logical justifications for your conclusions.

Speak No Evil

As you strive to manifest wealth and success, it's vital to eliminate limiting words like ‘can’t,’ ‘impossible,’ and ‘try’ from your vocabulary. Remember, each word you speak seeps into your subconscious, so be mindful of your language and speak with intent and caution.

Drawing from The Bible, the idea that God created reality through the ‘word’ is presented. As beings created in God's image, we too possess this ability. When using the phrase ‘I Am,’ recognize its significance - I Am strong, capable, efficient, courageous, purposeful, happy, joyful, and love. Conversely, avoid self - defeating statements that weaken your sense of self. Each ‘I Am’ statement leaves a powerful imprint on your subconscious, shaping your reality.

The Bible also reveals that when Moses asked for God's name, He replied, “I Am that I Am.” This emphasizes that we become what we claim to be. If you label yourself as lazy, that's what you'll be; if you call someone else stupid, their words will seem foolish to you. Be mindful of self - deprecating humor, as it can reinforce negative behavior in your subconscious. Instead, focus on honesty, spirit, and the potential for change.

When communicating, ensure your words align with your intentions. For instance, if you want to be a courageous salesperson, speak only of courage. Avoid qualifiers, such as "I'm courageous most of the time, except for when I must sell in a crowded room.” Eliminate any “buts” that undermine your goals. Even if you initially feel afraid, speaking your fear only reinforces and strengthens it. Embrace the idea of “I Am courageous when selling in public,” and you’ll soon find that this self - affirmation shifts from self - deception to reality, as it becomes ingrained in your subconscious mind.

Visualize

Visualization is a powerful technique often employed by elite athletes who, during their daily training, envision themselves achieving victory. The most decorated Olympian in history, swimmer Michael Phelps, exemplifies this approach:

“He’s the best I’ve ever seen and maybe the best ever in terms of visualization. He will see exactly the perfect race. He will see it like he is in the stands and he’ll see it like he’s in the water.” - Michael's coach, Bob Bowman

To harness the power of visualization, practice it frequently. Master the ability to construct a world around you, with elements entirely conceived in your mind. Imagine a stack of $100 bills on your desk that isn’t really there or even something as mundane as a non - existent stapler. Maintain control and consciousness in this process - this isn't about embracing madness. Just as you can choose to ignore an insult from a stranger you'll never encounter again, you can also choose to envision things both present and absent from your life.

If you seek confidence, visualize yourself exuding it. If you aim to be the greatest salesman of the 21st century, imagine yourself as that person, right now - not in the distant future. To become what you desire, engage your imagination and visualization.

The subconscious mind retains every experience and constantly strives to create a present that reflects your past. While it prioritizes recent events, it cannot differentiate between your visualizations and your actual experiences. By regularly visualizing a prosperous life, you can influence your subconscious mind's perception of your identity - essentially tricking it into believing that your imagined events have actually transpired. This is where imagination plays a crucial role: it empowers you to design a future that surpasses your past.

When visualizing, immerse yourself in the details. If you aim to manifest a multi - million - dollar small business, don't settle for a mere snapshot. Envision a three - dimensional experience where you can walk through the sales floor, interact with the products, engage with customers, and truly immerse yourself in that environment.

How To Visualize

Find a comfortable space where you can remain undisturbed for 10 minutes. Set an alarm to notify you when your time is up - a soothing song is ideal.

Determine the specific financial goal, success, or achievement you wish to visualize.

If possible, examine a picture of the environment where this accomplishment will occur (e.g., an image of the office building you aspire to own).

Close your eyes.

Deliberately slow your breathing by taking deep, measured breaths. Be mindful of your inhalations and exhalations.

Envision your body unwinding, one significant body part at a time, from head to toe: head, shoulders, arms, legs, and feet.

Continue breathing deeply, imagining each body part more relaxed than before.

Immerse yourself in the environment, conjuring as much detail as possible [Practice, as with all things, refines your skills - you can even visualize yourself visualizing to improve further.: - ) ]

When your alarm sounds, awaken and acknowledge your awesomeness.

As you practice, your visualizations will become increasingly vivid, briefly startling even your conscious mind upon awakening. When you reach the point where your conscious mind is momentarily immersed in the illusion, your focus and motivation will skyrocket. This is when your mind aligns, even if temporarily, allowing you to operate with grace, confidence, and unflappability. This state empowers you to take precise actions at the right moments, leading to wealth, power, and respect.

Create Courage

To create wealth, you must not allow fear to dominate your life. Confront your fears, as they obscure your connection to the subconscious. Fear activates the amygdala (a crucial part of the subconscious mind), which shifts the brain into fight or flight mode and releases adrenaline and cortisol. While beneficial in imminent danger, prolonged exposure to these hormones is detrimental to the body. Overcome fear by embracing possibility. Visualize yourself triumphing over adversity and continuing to flourish. Donald Trump, for instance, weathered financial storms and reemerged as a multi - billionaire for various arguable reasons. However, a key factor was his unwavering belief in his worth and his ability to negotiate from a position of confidence rather than fear or panic.

Meditation

As you forge a connection with your subconscious mind, you unlock the power to manifest wealth and, in essence, design the life of your dreams. Meditation serves as one of the most vital practices for tapping into this inner realm. In truth, each activity I've mentioned here is a form of meditation. Establishing this connection is fundamentally about quieting the conscious mind and attuning yourself to the whispers of your innermost, abundant self.

Meditation, being the most concentrated and organic means of connecting with the subconscious, revolves around attaining a state of silence. There's no need for financial affirmations or visualizations while meditating - only the pursuit of peace, bliss, and stillness. Incorporate meditation into your daily routine. Whether you meditate for a minute, ten, or fifteen minutes twice a day, you'll gradually find yourself in a state of centeredness, empowerment, and profound connection to infinite wealth.

Numerous studies have demonstrated the profound impact of meditation on various aspects of health - from cardiovascular and immune systems to psychological well - being, and even the recent discovery of its ability to extend cellular telomeres, effectively decelerating the aging process.

The more you practice meditation, the more rapidly you’ll be able to access a meditative state. I advocate for one - minute meditation sessions throughout your busiest days. If you feel you're too busy to meditate, that's precisely when you need it most.

How To Meditate

“Calmness is the ideal state in which we should receive all life's experiences.” - Paramahansa Yogananda

Meditation, akin to visualization and self - hypnosis, involves entering a tranquil state of mind. The fundamental distinction lies in the pursuit of complete silence during meditation, devoid of affirmations or visualizations. However, you may opt to conclude your meditative sessions with affirmations or visualizations to fully harness the power of that tranquil state.

Find a comfortable seated position in a space where you won't be interrupted for 10 minutes (use music or an alarm to signal the end of your session).

Gently close your eyes.

Deliberately slow your breathing, taking deep and controlled breaths.

Envision your body relaxing, focusing on each major body part sequentially from head to toe: head, shoulders, arms, legs, and feet.

Continue breathing deeply, imagining each body part becoming increasingly relaxed.

Concentrate on your breath, and should thoughts arise, acknowledge them before returning your focus to your breath.

Emerge from your meditation when the music ends or the timer sounds.

The brain primarily relies on oxygen and glucose from the blood, so enhanced cardiovascular health results in a more efficient and effective brain. Exercise and proper nutrition contribute to overall well - being, enabling one to operate at a heightened energy level and allowing the brain to communicate more effectively within itself. Additional Guidelines for Planting Wealth Building Ideas in the Subconscious

Frame ideas positively: “I swiftly recognize investment opportunities.” “I never overlook investment opportunities.”

Express ideas in past or present tense (avoid future tense, even when referencing future dates): “I earn one million dollars in real estate sales.” (Good) “I earned one million dollars in real estate sales.” (Good) “I am earning one million dollars in real estate sales.” (Good) “Between July 2020 (future date) and December 2021, I earned one million dollars in real estate sales.” (Good) “Between July 2020 (future date) and December 2021, I will earn one million dollars in real estate sales.”

Infuse emotions when planting ideas or visualizing. The subconscious mind responds to emotion. It influences the conscious mind by altering emotions, which forms the basis of human decision - making. We make decisions emotionally and justify them with logic. To control your behavior, reverse this effect: choose behaviors logically and justify them to the subconscious mind using emotion.

“The more intensely we feel about an idea or a goal, the more assuredly the idea, buried deep in our subconscious, will direct us along the path to its fulfillment.” - Earl Nightingale

Indeed, every thought plants itself in the subconscious mind. The more emotion associated with a thought, the more dominant it becomes in your subconscious, leading to more life experiences aligned with that thought.

Maintain the connection between the conscious and subconscious. Some people lessen or abandon meditation and self - hypnosis once they achieve key goals or gain momentum. They mistakenly attribute their success to the conscious mind - the ego - causing their progress to plateau or even regress. It's crucial to continue activities that impress the subconscious mind throughout your life. To ensure this, I strongly recommend planting these ideas in your subconscious:

i. “I engage in meditation/hypnosis at least twice daily, indefinitely.” ii. “I can access my subconscious mind during peaceful waking moments.”

These meditations create a self - reinforcing cycle, ensuring the entire process remains on track and effective.

Wealth Creation Should Be Easy

In the grand scheme of things, the work you do should feel effortless, enjoyable, and seamless. Struggling signifies that you're working against your subconscious rather than in harmony with it. This friction between the conscious and subconscious minds indicates that there's still much to be done in fortifying your connection with your subconscious. By enhancing this connection, you’ll find it easier to transform your self - image into one that is aligned with the pursuit of wealth and success.

When our unconscious and subconscious minds are at odds over their goals, we experience discomfort, cognitive dissonance, and a general sense of unease. This discord arises when the aspirations we consciously hold differ from those embedded within our subconscious. This internal conflict can make us feel out of control, akin to an addict grappling with their addiction. We feel powerless when we can't consciously influence our subconscious mind.

For the fortunate few, a well - programmed subconscious from childhood leads to a life of abundance. However, if you haven't been as lucky and have accumulated unproductive programming from family, friends, neighbors, or even television, fear not. You have the power to rewrite your subconscious mind's programming and embark on a journey towards a more focused and prosperous life.
