Date: 11/02/2030

From: Curator General A. Calise

To: Inner Council & Public Liaison Unit

Subject: Post - Crisis Transparency Measures (Spore Incident Follow - up)

Team,

Now that six months have passed since the [REDACTED] Spore Incident and public anxiety has fully abated, it is time to consider disclosure of certain details we withheld initially.

Our credibility long - term benefits from honesty once it’s safe. Specifically, I propose we quietly include in next quarter’s “State of Security” report to the Citizens’ Assembly:

- A mention that an attempted bio - attack occurred in April (already known in general, but we omitted extent).

- An admission that initial detection was delayed due to sensor calibration errors, but swift action still prevented breach.

- Steps taken since: upgraded sensors, new training for rapid response.

By framing it as lessons learned and improvements made, we turn a past vulnerability into a narrative of growth. This also undercuts any potential whistleblower or leak from framing it as a “cover - up.” We preempt that by owning up.

This approach aligns with Principle 2 and 4 of our Custodianship Principles.

Please prepare a draft of the relevant section of the report and circulate for review. Ensure language is factual, humble, and assures public that at no point was there actual exposure.

Signed: A. Calise
