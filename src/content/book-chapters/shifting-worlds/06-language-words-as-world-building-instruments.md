## Chapter Promise
This is one of the central chapters.

Words are presented as tools of creation because they organize perception, identity, memory, and action. A word is not just a label. It is a command to the nervous system.

"I am stuck" builds one world.

"I am between strategies" builds another.

"I failed" builds one world.

"I found the weak point in the model" builds another.

## Key Sections
- Inner speech as self-programming.
- Spoken language as social reality construction.
- The danger of careless labels.
- Affirmations as model installation, not empty slogans.
- Why "words create worlds" can be understood both psychologically and metaphysically.

## Practice: The Vocabulary Shift
The reader replaces limiting phrases:

"I have to" → "I choose to"

"I failed" → "I received data"

"I'm overwhelmed" → "I need sequence"

"This is impossible" → "This model has not found the path yet"

"I'm not that kind of person" → "I am becoming that kind of person"
