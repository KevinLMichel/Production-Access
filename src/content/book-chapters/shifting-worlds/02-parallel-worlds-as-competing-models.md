## Chapter Promise
This chapter connects directly to Michel's earlier framework.

A parallel world can be understood as a different possible trajectory made available through a different model of self and reality. The reader is not asked merely to "believe harder," but to notice that each belief opens certain actions and closes others.

## Key Sections
- The defeated world, the disciplined world, the creative world, the healed world.
- How small interpretive shifts create large life divergences.
- The "choice point": every moment where a model can be selected.
- The subconscious as a possibility processor: the part of the mind that scans options too numerous for conscious calculation.
- Why the ideal parallel world is not reached by wishing, but by repeatedly selecting the thoughts, words, emotions, and behaviors that belong to it.

## Practice: Three-World Simulation
For a current goal, the reader imagines:

The Default World: what happens if nothing changes.

The Fear World: what happens if fear runs the model.

The Ideal World: what happens if the best version of the self leads.

Then they ask: "What does the Ideal World version of me believe, practice, notice, ignore, say, and do?"
